# Autonomous Vehicles (AV) Integration in Modern Traffic Systems: A Survey

* **Supervisor:** Dr. Kourosh Ziarati
* **Academic Year:** 2021 - 2022

## Project Overview
This repository archives my undergraduate thesis project, which delivers a comprehensive literature review, analytical survey, and mathematical evaluation of state-of-the-art methodologies regarding the simulation, optimization, and behavioral analysis of Autonomous Vehicles (AVs) and Shared Autonomous Vehicles (SAVs) within modern urban transportation networks.

## Core Modeling Scales Analyzed
The research evaluates traffic simulation paradigms across three distinct levels of detail:
1. **Macroscopic Scale:** Reviewing continuum traffic models based on fluid dynamics analogies (e.g., LWR and ARZ models).
2. **Microscopic Scale (Cellular Automata):** Analyzing discrete, parallel rule-based systems to capture detailed vehicle behavior (e.g., speed updates with dynamic foresight parameters $k$).
3. **Mesoscopic Scale (Agent-Based Frameworks):** Evaluating hybrid frameworks that simulate individual agent logic coupled with aggregate probability distributions.

## Methodological Deep-Dives
The survey critically reviews and reconstructs the data pipelines of five seminal papers:
* **Cellular Automata Frameworks:** Investigating phase transitions and hysteresis loops in mixed traffic networks (AVs and Human-Driven Vehicles).
* **Network User Equilibrium via Variational Inequalities (VI):** Solving simultaneous path routing and spatial parking choice optimization problems under rigorous convergence criteria (evaluated on the Sioux Falls network infrastructure).
* **Multi-Agent Transport Simulation (MATSim):** Evaluating Dynamic Ride-Sharing (DRS), optimal fleet sizing, and operator internal rates of return (IRR) utilizing real-world trip distribution demand matrices (Austin, Texas dataset).
* **Discrete Choice & Demand Modeling:** Examining the spatial shifts in parking demand from central business districts (CBD) to peripheral suburbs, using mesoscopic traffic state simulators and distance-based pricing tolls (Melbourne, Australia case study).
